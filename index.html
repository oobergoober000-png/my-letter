As I’m writing this, I’m realizing how rarely I take the time to collect my thoughts and actually put them into words for you. I wanted to do something different and show you some effort, so I’m giving you this letter in a way that I hope feels thoughtful and intentional. I’m truly sorry for how I’ve been treating you, especially lately. Instead of participating in difficult conversations and trying to understand you, I’ve been avoidant and dismissive. As you know, my instinct is generally to shut down or pull away when I’m uncomfortable, and I know that is not fair to you. You deserve more than silence when things are tough. I also know I have not made you feel as appreciated as you should. I haven’t always made the effort to listen, to understand, or to let you into my life in the way you have tried to let me into yours. I need to be better about managing my emotions and not taking it out on you or others. That is something I am working on for myself, but it is also something I owe to you if I want to be someone you can rely on. Being here in grad school has made me think a lot about the kind of partner I’ve been to you. I really appreciate how understanding you have been through all of this. I can see how hard it must be to be in a relationship that does not always feel supportive or available, where it is not easy to depend on me or share your life with me in the way you should be able to. These past couple of weeks have pushed me to a very dark place mentally, and I let that spill over in a way that hurt you too. I know now that I have not always been as present or as considerate as I should have been, and I want to be more fully a part of your life. I’ve also been thinking about the postcards, letters, and other thoughtful things you’ve given me over time. Although I don't do a good job showing you my appreciation, you have constantly made me feel known and loved. I just hope I can make you feel the same way. I understand how frustrating it must have been to feel like you were the one always trying to start conversations or make things better. I know I have a lot to work on, but you matter enough to me that I want to do what it takes. I can’t undo the ways I’ve hurt you or the times I’ve made you feel unappreciated, but I hope this letter can be a real step toward doing better. I do love you, and I want you to feel understood, cared for, and happy. More than anything, I want you to know that I am serious about trying.
